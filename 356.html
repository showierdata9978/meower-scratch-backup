Which option sounds most fitted for this small, unprofessional project?

As of now, we do explicitly state what we do need to collect (which is absolute barebones to make things work, we don't collect sensitive information, and we don't track users) and if anyone was really skeptical about the project they can always get in contact with me or look at the source code.

But, the suggestion for hiring a proper legal team should be imperative for the future…

I'm no professional when it comes to the law (though I try my best to be as educated as I can… conveniently taking a Personal Law / Business Law class right now!), but from what I can conclude here's a basic outline of what we need to follow:

1. We will explicitly ask for permission to store data to make the service work (GDPR, Meower's ToS/PP Consent Screen prior to Account Creation)
2. We will offer the rights to our users' data and must provide services to manage that data (GDPR)
3. We cannot collect personally identifying information or track our users over the internet, especially minors (Pretty much common sense, also COPPA)
4. For minors, we will ask for permission from parents to use the service (COPPA)
5. We will remain transparent, FOSS, and privacy-friendly as possible (Play nice)
6. We will comply with local legislation (Since I own and manage Meower Media Co, and operate within the US, we will comply with the laws in the US)
7. We cannot discriminate any communication, but we are to moderate communications to keep our service friendly to all
8. In the event that policies are revised, we are to notify users and obtain consent to continue using the services

(TBH, getting a legal team sooner than later sound like a better idea to me now: I'm starting to get really, really tired of dealing with legalities…)
