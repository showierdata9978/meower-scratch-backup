The privacy policy I've shared on our forum is all-encompassing and doesn't include any hidden policies or info. What you see is what you get.

If you're a type of person who doesn't like legal jargon, I'll give you a TL;DR version of our Privacy Policy.

We only collect data that is needed to make our service work, which includes:

> A username and password for authentication, as well as account security. (This should be pretty standard.)

> Posts, messages, comments, threads, anything that is a form of messaging needs that to be saved/loaded. (Kinda need to be able to store data to get data.)

> Basic diagnostic data to help correct troubles as well as anonymously generate statistics. (How many people are using Meower today?)

> Record posts/messages/comments/threads for moderation purposes. (Did someone say a no-no word?)
Whatever happens to your data, we handle it ethically.

> We don't collect data that can be used to identify who you are in real life, and we don't sell our data to anyone. (If you're really keen on seeing how we do this, there's a reason why we're FOSS)

> If you desire to view (or delete) your diagnostic data, you can let us know directly.

> If you feel the need to, you can request to have your account (as well as diagnostic data and posts/messages/comments/threads) deleted.
And, if you feel skeptical about the aforementioned, anyone and everyone is more than welcome to view the source code. Meower is 100% free, and open source. If you would like to suggest an improvement to our policies, feel free to let us know!

Edit: I saw your concerns regarding data collection. It's my fault for not being specific about it. As I mentioned above, only the messages you post will be collected. Whoever's threads you view, posts you look at, or the people you just notice, will not be logged. Data collection is strictly limited to messages/posts/comments/threads, accounts, and diagnostics (which includes the date/time of account creation or a login/logout attempt).
